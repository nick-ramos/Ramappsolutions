The Problem

Real estate agents already have the information — address, bedrooms, bathrooms, square footage, features, neighborhood highlights, price. The problem is that they have to turn that same information into eight different pieces of content, each with a different format, length, and tone.

On average, agents were spending 2+ hours per listing on content that could have been generated in seconds:

  • Writing an MLS description that's compelling and within character limits
  • Drafting a marketing email for their contact list
  • Creating social media captions for Instagram, Facebook, and X — each formatted differently
  • Writing follow-up SMS texts for leads who inquired
  • Crafting a neighborhood overview for buyers unfamiliar with the area
  • Keeping their agent bio fresh and relevant

The quality was also inconsistent — some listings got polished copy, others got rushed descriptions. There was no system.

The Solution

ListingOS gives agents a single structured form. They fill in the listing details once — address, bedrooms, bathrooms, square footage, key features, neighborhood, price, and a tone preference (professional, warm, luxury, etc.). They hit submit.

That data is sent to an AI engine that understands real estate copy conventions. Within 30 seconds, it returns eight distinct, ready-to-use content pieces — each optimized for its specific channel and format. No editing required, though agents can tweak anything they want.

The tool is built for individual agents, brokerages, and real estate teams. At scale, the time savings compound quickly — a brokerage handling 20 listings a month recovers 40+ hours of agent time that goes directly back into selling.
