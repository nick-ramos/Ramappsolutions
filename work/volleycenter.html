The Problem

Volleyball clubs — even mid-sized ones — were drowning in administrative overhead. Coaches were managing rosters in Google Sheets, schedules in one app, payments in another, and parent communication across three different group chats. There was no single source of truth, and everyone was constantly out of sync.

  • Coaches wasting hours each week on admin tasks instead of coaching
  • Parents out of the loop on schedules, tournament details, and team updates
  • No structured way to plan and document practices or build a drill library
  • Payment collection scattered across Venmo, email, and paper forms
  • Zero visibility into facility availability, staff hours, or club-wide expenses

The clubs we studied were using 5+ separate tools. Every handoff between those tools was a gap where things fell through.

The Solution

VolleyCenter consolidates everything into a single platform with role-based access for four user types: Admins, Coaches, Players, and Parents. Each role sees exactly what they need — nothing more, nothing less.

Admins manage the club at the macro level: finances, rosters, staff, facility reservations, and registration events with payment collection. Coaches have dedicated tools for building practice plans, scheduling, designing plays, and communicating with their teams. Players access their schedules and resources. Parents stay informed without being overwhelmed.

The platform is built for real clubs with real complexity — not a generic sports app with a volleyball logo slapped on it.

🧠

AI Feature Highlight

AI-Generated Practice Plans

A coach inputs the team's age group, the focus area for the session (serving, passing, game situations, etc.), and the practice duration. VolleyCenter's AI engine builds a complete, time-blocked practice plan — drill by drill — with descriptions, coaching cues, and timing for each segment. What used to take 30–45 minutes of planning takes under a minute. Plans can be saved to the library and reused or modified for future sessions.
